Mr. Ratan Tata

The Man .. The Myth.. The Legend..

Here's a time line of Mr. Ratan Tata's life:

  • 1937 - December 28: Ratan Naval Tata, an Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former Chairman of Tata Sons, is born in Mumbai, India. He was born into the renowned Tata family, which has played a significant role in shaping India's industrial landscape.
  • 1962: After completing his education, Ratan Tata graduates with a degree in Architecture from Cornell University in the United States. Although he had an early interest in architecture and design, destiny called him to join the family business, the Tata Group.
  • 1962: Ratan Tata officially begins his career at the Tata Group, where he gains valuable experience on the shop floor of Tata Steel. This hands-on approach allowed him to understand the inner workings of the company and its various operations.
  • 1971: Ratan Tata's dedication and commitment to the Tata Group's growth lead him to be appointed as the Director of National Radio & Electronics (NELCO), a Tata company. This marked the beginning of his journey into leadership roles within the conglomerate.
  • 1977: Ratan Tata takes on the role of Chairman of Tata Industries, a crucial position that helps him to spearhead several strategic ventures and collaborations for the Tata Group, both in India and internationally.
  • 1981: Continuing his ascent within the organization, Ratan Tata assumes the Chairmanship of Tata Industries. Under his guidance, the Tata Group diversifies its interests, exploring new industries and international markets.
  • 1991: A significant milestone in his career, Ratan Tata is appointed as the Chairman of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. This appointment comes after the retirement of the legendary J.R.D. Tata, who had been at the helm for over 50 years.
  • 1998: An iconic moment for the Indian automotive industry, Tata Motors, under Ratan Tata's visionary leadership, introduces the Tata Indica, India's first fully indigenous car. This affordable and reliable hatchback revolutionizes the country's automotive landscape.
  • 2000: Tata Tea, one of the Tata Group's key subsidiaries, makes a significant global acquisition under Ratan Tata's strategic guidance. Tata Tea acquires Tetley, a British tea company, becoming the world's second-largest branded tea company and expanding its global presence.
  • 2007: In a landmark move, Tata Motors, under Ratan Tata's leadership, acquires the prestigious British car brands Jaguar and Land Rover (JLR) from Ford Motor Company. This acquisition elevates Tata Motors to an international player in the automotive industry.
  • 2008: Ratan Tata receives the Padma Bhushan, one of India's highest civilian awards, in recognition of his outstanding contributions to industry and philanthropy. This award is a testament to his exceptional leadership and commitment to social causes.
  • 2012: After an illustrious tenure as Chairman, Ratan Tata announces his retirement from Tata Sons. Under his leadership, the Tata Group expanded globally, diversified into new sectors, and solidified its reputation as a socially responsible business conglomerate.
  • 2016: In a surprising turn of events, Ratan Tata returns as the Interim Chairman of Tata Sons following the sudden removal of Cyrus Mistry. His return to the top leadership role is seen as a stabilizing force during a challenging time for the Tata Group.
  • 2017: Ratan Tata steps down from his position as Interim Chairman, and N. Chandrasekaran takes over as the Chairman of Tata Sons. Ratan Tata's contributions to the Tata Group and his inspiring leadership have left a lasting legacy, influencing the company's values and vision.
